# Korean Learning Suggestions

Below is a concise roadmap you can follow to start learning Korean efficiently. It is organized by resource type, daily schedule, and progress‑tracking ideas.

---

## 1. Build the Foundations

| Area | Recommended Resources | Why it helps |
|------|----------------------|--------------|
| **Alphabet (Hangul)** | • YouTube: “Learn Hangul in 5 minutes” <br>• Anki deck: *Hangul Basics* | Hangul is phonetic and can be mastered in a few days – essential before anything else. |
| **Basic Grammar** | • **Integrated Korean** (Series 1 & 2) – textbook + workbook <br>• **Talk To Me In Korean (TTMIK)** – Lesson 1‑10 (free) | Structured explanations with clear exercises; TTMIK adds audio for pronunciation. |
| **Vocabulary** | • **Memrise** or **LingoDeer** (daily 10‑15 min) <br>• **Anki** decks: *Top 1000 Korean words* | Spaced‑repetition ensures long‑term retention. |

## 2. Everyday Exposure

| Modality | How to Do It |
|----------|--------------|
| **Listening** | • TTMIK podcasts (slow, beginner‑friendly) <br>• Korean dramas or variety shows with Korean subtitles (skip English subtitles as you progress) |
| **Reading** | • Webtoons with Korean text (easy, visual context) <br>• Naver news headlines (short, high‑frequency vocab) |
| **Speaking** | • **HelloTalk** / **Tandem** – find native speakers for 5‑minute voice exchanges <br>• Record yourself reading sentences and compare to native audio |
| **Writing** | • Practice Hangul handwriting on paper or a digital app <br>• Use **Lang‑8** or **HiNative** for feedback on short journal entries |

## 3. Structured Study Schedule (20‑30 min daily)

| Time Block | Activity |
|------------|----------|
| 5 min | Review Anki flashcards (spaced‑repetition) |
| 10 min | One grammar lesson (Integrated Korean or TTMIK) + short workbook exercise |
| 5 min | Listening practice (podcast clip or drama episode) |
| 5‑10 min | Speak/write: shadow a sentence, then try it yourself; or send a voice note on HelloTalk |
| **Optional** (once/week) | Attend a local Korean meetup or a virtual language‑exchange session (30‑60 min) |

## 4. Immersion & Motivation

- **Music** – Follow K‑pop artists you like; look up the lyrics and sing along.
- **Cooking** – Try Korean recipes (kimchi, bibimbap) while reading the instructions in Korean.
- **News** – Set up a Naver news RSS feed; skim headlines each morning.

## 5. Track Progress & Adjust

| Metric | Tool |
|--------|------|
| Vocabulary size | Anki stats (cards due per day) |
| Grammar coverage | Checklist of Integrated Korean chapters completed |
| Speaking confidence | Weekly self‑assessment (rate 1‑5) + number of voice exchanges |
| Listening comprehension | Ability to understand a short drama scene without subtitles |

Re‑evaluate every 2‑4 weeks: add new resources, increase session length, or focus on a weaker skill (e.g., more speaking if you’re good at reading).

---

### Quick‑Start Checklist
1. Install **Anki** and download a Hangul + basic vocab deck.
2. Set up a **Memrise** or **LingoDeer** account and commit to a daily streak.
3. Grab the first two books of **Integrated Korean** (or order the PDF if physical copies aren’t handy).
4. Subscribe to the **TTMIK** YouTube channel; watch Lesson 1 today.
5. Create a HelloTalk profile and send a “Hello, I’m learning Korean!” intro message.

**Tip:** Consistency beats intensity. A solid 20‑minute habit is far more effective than a single 3‑hour binge.
